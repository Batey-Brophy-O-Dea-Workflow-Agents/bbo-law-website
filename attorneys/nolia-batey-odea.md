## The Short Version

I have spent the last ten years helping clients navigate some of the hardest moments of their lives.  Car accidents, trucking wrecks, catastrophic injuries, business disputes, domestic violence, high-conflict divorces -- these are the cases where people need someone who will fight for them and actually pick up the phone.

Before I became an attorney, I spent seven years as a paralegal.  That experience changed everything about how I practice law.  I learned how to support injured clients, manage complex files, and fight insurance companies -- from the inside out.  By the time I got to law school, I already knew what it looked like when a case was handled well and when it was not.

Many of my clients stay in touch long after their case is over.  That matters to me more than anything on a resume.

## Background

Before law school, I spent seven years as a paralegal, learning firsthand what injured clients go through -- the paperwork, the insurance fights, the waiting.  When I finished law school, I went straight into practice with all of that behind me.  I have been on every side of the process, and I know where things fall through the cracks.

During law school, I worked in-house with Churchill Downs, Inc. in their legal and compliance department and later with the IRS Small Business/Self-Employed Division.  Those experiences gave me an inside look at regulatory compliance, investigations, and how large institutions operate -- knowledge I still use every day when I am going up against an insurance company or a corporate defendant.

## What I Do

### Personal Injury

This is the heart of my practice.  Catastrophic personal injury cases, car accidents, trucking accidents -- the cases where someone got hurt and the insurance company is already working against them.

I have been fighting insurance companies since my paralegal days.  I know their playbook.  Kentucky has its own rules -- we are a no-fault state with PIP insurance requirements, a comparative negligence system, and statutes of limitation that vary depending on the type of case.  I work inside these rules every single day.

### Business Disputes

Business litigation requires patience, strategy, and a clear-eyed understanding of what is actually at stake.  I handle disputes where the path forward is not obvious and the other side is not going to make it easy.

### Domestic Violence and High-Conflict Family Law

I handle domestic violence litigation and high-asset, high-conflict divorces.  These cases are deeply personal, and they require an attorney who understands that the legal strategy has to account for the human reality.  I try to make the process as straightforward as possible -- not because it is simple, but because adding confusion to an already painful situation helps nobody.

## Community and Pro Bono Work

I currently serve as Chair of a Citizen Foster Care Review Board, where community members review foster care cases to make sure children in the system are not falling through the cracks.

I dedicate significant time to pro bono work for both organizations and individuals.  I have stayed actively involved with the Kentucky Bar Association since the start of my career, and I am a member of the Kentucky Justice Association.
