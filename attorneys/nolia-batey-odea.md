## The Short Version

I became a lawyer because I care about people who are going through the worst day of their life.  Not in a brochure way -- in a "pick up the phone on a Saturday" way.  I focus on personal injury, family law, and animal law, and I have been practicing in Louisville for years.

If you have been hurt in an accident, are going through a divorce, or need someone who actually understands animal law in Kentucky -- that is what I do every day.

## Education

I went to Western Kentucky University for my undergraduate degree, then to the University of Louisville's Louis D. Brandeis School of Law for my J.D.  After law school, I went back for an LL.M. in animal law from Lewis & Clark Law School in Portland, Oregon -- one of the only programs of its kind in the country.

I did that because I believe the law should protect everyone who cannot protect themselves.  That principle runs through everything I do, whether the client is a person recovering from a car wreck or a family navigating the worst chapter of their lives.

## What I Do

### Personal Injury

This is the heart of my practice.  Car accidents, slip and falls, premises liability, dog bites -- the cases where someone got hurt because someone else was not careful enough.

Kentucky has its own rules.  We are a no-fault state with PIP insurance requirements.  We have a comparative negligence system.  The statute of limitations is different depending on the type of case.  I know these rules because I work inside them every single day.

### Family Law

Divorce is hard.  There is no way around it.  I handle contested and uncontested divorces, custody matters, and property division.  I try to make the process as straightforward as possible -- not because it is simple, but because adding confusion to an already painful situation helps nobody.

### Animal Law

I hold an LL.M. in animal law, which makes me one of the few attorneys in Kentucky with advanced training in this area.  This background shapes how I think about the law more broadly -- it taught me to advocate for those who do not have a voice of their own.

## Community

I chair the Jefferson County Citizen Foster Care Review Board.  This is a volunteer position where community members review foster care cases to make sure children in the system are not falling through the cracks.  It is one of the things I am proudest of in my career.
